Parent (Migrant) Visa (Subclass 103)
Reunite with your children and build a future in Australia with the Subclass 103 visa.

The Parent (Migrant) Visa (Subclass 103) is designed for parents of Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens who wish to live in Australia permanently with their children.

Key Benefits

  • • Permanent residency in Australia.
  • • Opportunity to live with and be reunited with children in Australia.
  • • Access to Australian healthcare and social services.
  • • Eligible family members can be included in the application.

Eligibility

  • • Be sponsored by an eligible child who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen.
  • • Meet health and character requirements.
  • • Meet the balance-of-family test.

Common Pathways

  • • Parents of Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens who wish to live with their children in Australia often choose this visa.

Duration and Validity

The Parent (Migrant) Visa (Subclass 103) is a permanent visa, allowing you to live and work in Australia indefinitely.

Application Process Overview

  • • Be sponsored by an eligible child who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen.
  • • Meet the balance-of-family test, which requires at least half of your children to be Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens, or more of your children to be in Australia than in any other single country.
  • • Gather necessary documents, including proof of dependency.
  • • Apply for the visa online, providing all required information.
  • • Pay the application fee.
  • • Attend a health examination and provide character documents, if required.
  • • Wait for your visa to be processed (processing times vary).
  • • Once approved, you can live with your children in Australia as a permanent resident.

For detailed information on the Parent (Migrant) Visa (Subclass 103), including eligibility criteria, application procedures, and more, please contact us or visit us at our nearest office.
